# Pricing Structure

Three tiers. All fixed-price, not hourly. Fixed price protects both sides — you from scope creep, them from an open-ended invoice.

---

## Tier 1: Audit + Architecture

**Price:** $3,000–$8,000
**Duration:** 1–2 weeks
**What they get:** Written assessment of their current setup, recommended agent architecture, prioritized gap list, go/no-go recommendation

**When to use this price range:**
- $3k: Small team (under 20 people), simple design system, early Storybook setup
- $5k: Mid-size team, established tokens and Storybook, some AI tooling already in use
- $8k: Larger team, complex multi-brand system, existing automation that needs to be audited too

The audit fee gets credited toward the build engagement if they proceed. This removes the "why am I paying to be sold something" objection.

---

## Tier 2: Build + Deploy

**Price:** $15,000–$30,000
**Duration:** 6–8 weeks
**What they get:** Running agentic design layer, documentation, team walkthrough, 30 days of post-launch support

**When to use this price range:**
- $15k: Scoped engagement — one workflow (e.g., component spec → Storybook story only), clean existing token system
- $20k–$25k: Full handoff workflow with token contract enforcement, Storybook connection, and guardrail definitions
- $30k: Multi-brand or complex system, multiple workflow paths, extended team training

**What's not included:**
- Redesigning their design system (that's a different engagement)
- Building their Storybook from scratch (they need one that works first)
- Ongoing maintenance (that's Tier 3)

---

## Tier 3: Monthly Retainer

**Price:** $2,000–$4,000/month
**Minimum term:** 3 months
**What they get:** System maintenance, prompt updates, new guardrail additions, monthly check-in, 48-hour support response

**When to use this price range:**
- $2k: Stable system, occasional updates, low support volume
- $3k: Active design system growth, regular new component additions, moderate support
- $4k: High-velocity team, significant AI tooling changes underway, priority response needed

---

## Common objections and responses

**"Can we start with just the retainer and skip the build?"**
No. The retainer maintains a system. There's nothing to maintain until the build is done.

**"Can we do this hourly?"**
I don't do hourly for this work. The value is the output — a running system — not the time. Hourly billing incentivizes you to watch the clock; fixed price incentivizes me to deliver efficiently.

**"This is more than we budgeted."**
Start with the audit only. It's a contained risk, you own the output either way, and it tells you whether a build engagement is worth the investment. If the audit shows the gap isn't there, you've spent $5k to find that out — which is worth it.

**"Can you do the audit for free?"**
No. The audit is a deliverable — a written document you own. It has real value independent of whether you hire me for the build. Free audits create misaligned incentives.

## Payment terms

- Audit: 50% on signing, 50% on delivery
- Build: 30% on signing, 30% at midpoint, 40% on delivery
- Retainer: Monthly invoices, net 15

Late payment: 1.5% per month after 30 days.
