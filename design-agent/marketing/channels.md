# Where to Market

Goal: 3 paying clients in 90 days. Cast wide, convert narrow.

The target is a Head of Design or VP Engineering at a company with 30–200 people, a Figma + Storybook setup, and a design system that's always slightly out of sync. They're feeling the pain of Claude Design / Figma AI generating things that don't match their system. They don't have a design engineer on staff.

---

## Tier 1: Highest ROI (do these first)

### Direct network outreach
Every person from past client work, agency relationships, and conference contacts who is now at a company that fits the ICP. One message. Not a pitch — a question. "Are you running into X problem?" Three warm conversations beat fifty cold emails.

### LinkedIn (content + DM)
Post one piece of thinking per week on the design systems + AI topic. Not generic takes — specific observations from real work (the guardrail problem, what breaks when Figma AI generates components outside the token system, what agent cards actually look like in production). Each post should make a technical design lead stop scrolling. After each post, DM the 5–10 people who engaged with something specific and relevant, not "let's connect."

Profiles to target for DMs:
- Head of Design / VP Design at Series A–C companies
- Staff Design Engineer / Principal Designer
- VP Engineering at companies with a known design system (check their job listings — if they're hiring for "design system" or "design engineer," they have the problem)

### Design Systems Community Slack
Post in #jobs-and-gigs and #general at the right moment (not spam — contribute first). This community has the exact ICP. Config, Clarity, and Schema conference attendees are all in here.

---

## Tier 2: Medium ROI (set up, then let compound)

### Writing distribution
Every article that lives on ramphal.design should also live on:
- Substack (for the email list — this is the long game)
- LinkedIn articles (for algorithm reach)
- Dev.to or Hashnode (for the engineering-adjacent audience)

The "When UX Becomes AX" piece and whatever gets written about "what a design agent needs to know" are the two highest-signal pieces for this market.

### Conference speaking
Config (Figma), Clarity (design systems), Schema (Figma DS-focused), Smashing Conf. A talk titled "I built a design agent for my team — here's what it actually does" is a credible pitch for any of these. Speaking converts better than any other channel because the audience self-selects.

Priority: submit to Clarity and Schema. Both are smaller and more targeted than Config. Easier to get on the program, more qualified audience for this service.

### Referrals from agency/consulting network
Orium and adjacent agencies regularly turn away or refer out work that doesn't fit their model. A side arrangement where you get referrals for standalone design system + AI work is worth having explicitly.

---

## Tier 3: Lower ROI (skip for now)

- Cold email blasts — low conversion, burns reputation
- Twitter/X — wrong audience for B2B design engineering services
- Dribbble / Behance — visual design audience, not design systems buyers
- SEO / content marketing — 6–12 month lag before any signal
- Product Hunt — only relevant if there's a self-serve product to launch

---

## The message that works

Not: "I offer agentic design services."

Instead: describe the specific pain. Something like —

> "Design teams using Figma AI and Claude Design are generating components that don't match their system. The components look right but break the token contract, skip the accessibility rules, and don't exist in Storybook. I build the layer that catches that before it hits the codebase."

That's a specific problem. Anyone with a design system and AI tooling in their team recognizes it immediately.
