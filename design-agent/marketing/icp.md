# Ideal Customer Profile

Who to target, what they care about, and how to recognize them.

---

## The buyer

**Primary:** Head of Design, VP Design, or Principal/Staff Designer at a product company
**Secondary:** VP Engineering or CTO at a company where design/dev handoff is a known bottleneck

They have budget authority or can make the case to someone who does. They've probably already tried to solve this internally and it didn't stick.

---

## Company profile

| Signal | Details |
|---|---|
| Size | 30–200 employees (big enough to need this, small enough to not have a full design engineering team) |
| Stage | Series A through C, or a profitable mid-size product company |
| Stack | Figma + Storybook (or equivalent component library) + some token implementation |
| Pain | Design and dev are always out of sync. Handoff is manual or semi-automated and keeps breaking down. |
| AI status | They're experimenting with Figma AI, Claude Design, or similar — and starting to realize it generates things that don't match their system. |
| Hiring signals | Job posts for "design engineer," "design systems," or "front-end design" — they know they have the gap, they're trying to hire their way out of it |

---

## What they've already tried

- Gave designers tokens and told them to use them — adoption was inconsistent
- Built a Storybook and told devs to use it — it drifted from design within a month
- Tried a design system working group — it helped for a while, then someone left
- Are now watching AI tools generate components that look right but break the system

---

## What they actually want

They want the gap between Figma and the codebase to stop being their problem. They want to ship faster without the design quality regressing. They want AI tooling that reinforces their system rather than bypassing it.

They will not use the words "agentic design layer." They will say things like: "our design system is always out of date," "devs and designers are always out of sync," "we shipped something last week that wasn't in the design," "Figma AI keeps generating components that don't exist in our library."

---

## Disqualifiers (don't pursue)

- No Storybook and no appetite to build one — wrong stage
- Design of one (no team) — not enough complexity to justify the engagement
- Agency model (client work) — needs are different, timeline doesn't fit
- Already has a full-time design engineer on staff — they'll build it themselves
- Pre-product-market-fit startup — too early, wrong priorities

---

## Where to find them

- LinkedIn: search "Head of Design" + company size filter + "design systems" or "Figma" in their activity
- Design Systems Community Slack: #intros, #general, people asking questions about Figma AI integration
- Job boards: companies posting "design engineer" or "design systems" roles — they have the problem and might prefer to solve it with a consultant first
- Conference attendee lists: Clarity, Schema, Config
