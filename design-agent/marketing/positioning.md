## The problem

Most design teams are understaffed. One designer covering ground that used to take two or three. AI design tools are making that worse in a specific way: Figma AI, Claude Design, and similar tools generate components faster than any team can evaluate them — output that looks right but skips the token contract, misses the accessibility rules, and doesn't exist in Storybook. Design debt at AI speed.

The bottleneck isn't the AI. It's the layer between the AI and the system — the guardrails, the token contract, the handoff definition. Most teams don't have that layer. The design system lives in a Notion page. The handoff is a Loom video. The guardrails are a Slack message to the dev who remembers.

---

## The service

I design and deploy agentic design layers for product teams.

An AI-powered workflow that sits between your designers and your codebase. It applies your token system, checks against your component library, flags what needs a human before it hits a pull request, and produces structured handoff specs that developers can act on without a meeting.

The result: one designer covering what used to require two or three. Not because the designer is working harder — because the mechanical layer is handled, and their judgment is reserved for the decisions that actually require it.

This isn't a tool you subscribe to. It's a system built for your stack, your tokens, your Storybook, your team's workflow. I build it, deploy it, and stay on as the operator who keeps it calibrated.

---

## How it's deployed

**Embedded in your workflow**
Agent integrates into the tools your team already uses — Figma, Storybook, Slack, or your CI pipeline. Designers interact with it directly. Output flows into your existing handoff process.

**Fractional oversight**
Once the system is running, I monitor it across your team's usage, handle edge cases the agent can't resolve, tune prompts as your design system evolves, and flag decisions that need human judgment before they proceed. You get a senior design engineer on call without the headcount cost.

## Who this is for

Product teams with:
- An existing design system (Figma + tokens + Storybook or equivalent)
- A designer or small design team that's stretched thin
- AI tooling in use or planned — and a growing concern about output quality

Not a fit for: teams with no component library, pre-product-market-fit startups, or teams that already have a full design engineering function.

## Why this, why now

AI tools are not slowing down. Claude Design launched today and people are already calling it a Figma killer. The teams that build the infrastructure to integrate these tools into a disciplined system will ship faster with less regression. The teams that don't will generate design debt faster than they can spend it.

The bottleneck has never been the AI. It's always been the judgment layer — who decides what's right, what gets flagged, what ships. That layer requires design knowledge and engineering knowledge at the same time. Most teams don't have both in one person. I do, and I've built it into deployable infrastructure.
