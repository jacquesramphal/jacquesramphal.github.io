# Imposter syndrome never fully goes away

I'm writing this because I'm in it right now. So if you're reading this and you know the feeling, hopefully this helps.

## The loop

The loop goes like this: you prepare, you show up, and you leave the room convinced you had the wrong answer. So next time you prepare differently, or over-prepare, and spend the drive home replaying the conversation looking for what you missed. The goalpost keeps moving no matter how much ground you cover.

What actually helps is stopping trying to prepare for the verdict and focusing on showing up with intention instead: clear on why you're there, paying real attention rather than waiting for your turn, not staging for a result. That means releasing ego in real time, not rambling, asking the question you don't know the answer to instead of talking around it. "I don't know, let me find out" is a complete answer.

Learning to think on your feet is a different skill from knowing things. It helps to pause, identify the actual question being asked, then answer that and nothing more, without bullshitting or filling the silence. It won't feel like enough, but do it anyway.

## After

After, the internal audit starts. You remember the two things that didn't land and forget the eight that did, which isn't a useful review so much as threat detection running as self-assessment. The order matters: note what went right first, then look at the gap. The gap is real and worth closing, but close it from what's working, not from deficit.

When feedback comes, hear what's actually true, not the emotion around it or the worst interpretation of it. Your work is not the full measure of your value.

---

Some discomfort means you care, and caring is part of doing good work. But if it's following you home and sitting at the table with your family, something is out of balance. The work will get done. Decide how much you can genuinely give and give that fully, then check in often: is this effort aimed at the right problem, or am I just adding hours?

Be kind to yourself, if only to build that practice now so the next time you need it, the support is already there.
