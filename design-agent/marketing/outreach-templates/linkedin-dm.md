# LinkedIn DM Templates

These are conversation starters, not pitches. The goal of the first message is one thing: a reply. Don't pitch in the first message.

---

## Template A — Problem recognition (use after engaging with their content)

> Saw your post about [specific thing — Figma AI, design system drift, handoff problems]. We ran into exactly that on [vague reference to relevant work]. Curious — are you finding it's a tooling problem or more of a process/adoption one?

Wait for reply. If they engage: share one specific thing you learned, ask one follow-up question. Let them qualify themselves.

---

## Template B — Shared context (mutual connection or community)

> We're both in [Design Systems Community / Config attendees / etc.] — noticed you're at [Company] working on [design systems / Figma rollout / whatever is visible on their profile]. Are you running into the AI-generated-components-that-don't-match-the-system problem yet? Feels like it's hitting a lot of teams right now.

This works because it names a real problem without positioning anything. If they say yes, you have a warm conversation.

---

## Template C — Direct, for warm leads (people you've worked with or met)

> Hey [name] — have been building something I think you'd find useful given where [Company] is at. Not a pitch — just would love 20 minutes to show you what it does and get your reaction. Worth a call?

Only use this with people who already know your work. The trust has to be there first.

---

## Template D — Cold, senior buyer (Head of Design / VP Eng you don't know)

> [Name] — I've been following [Company]'s design system work. Built something that I think addresses a gap most teams hit when they introduce AI tooling into their design workflow — specifically the disconnect between what Figma AI/Claude Design generates and what actually lives in the token system and Storybook. Would a 20-minute conversation be worth your time?

Keep this short. Senior buyers get flooded. One specific problem, one ask.

---

## Follow-up (if no reply after 1 week)

> Following up in case this got buried — happy to send a one-pager if easier than a call.

One follow-up only. If no reply, move on.

---

## What not to do

- Don't attach a PDF or send a link in the first message
- Don't list features or services upfront
- Don't use "I help design teams..." as an opener — it reads as a pitch immediately
- Don't follow up more than once on a cold message
