## The problem

AI design tools are generating components faster than teams can evaluate them. Figma AI, Claude Design, and similar tools produce output that looks right — but skips the token contract, misses the accessibility rules, and doesn't exist in Storybook. The result is design debt at AI speed.

Most teams don't have the infrastructure to catch this. The design system is a Notion page and a Figma library. The handoff is a Loom video. The guardrails are a Slack message to the dev who remembers.

---

## The service

I design and deploy agentic design layers for product teams.

Concretely: an AI-powered workflow that sits between your designers and your codebase. It applies your token system, checks against your component library, and flags the decisions that need a human before they hit a pull request. It's your design system, encoded as agent behavior.

This isn't a tool you subscribe to. It's a system built for your stack, your tokens, your Storybook setup, your team's workflow. I build it, deploy it, and stay on to maintain it.

---

## What you get

**Audit + Architecture** (precedes every engagement)
A structured review of your current design system, token implementation, and handoff workflow. Delivered as a written report with a recommended agent architecture and an honest assessment of what will and won't transfer to automation.

**Build + Deploy**
Design and implementation of the agentic layer: prompt engineering, token contract integration, Storybook connection, guardrail definitions, handoff workflow. Delivered as a running system with documentation and a trained team.

**Ongoing maintenance**
Monthly retainer to extend the system, update prompts as AI tooling evolves, and add new guardrails as your design system grows.

---

## Why this, why now

AI design tools are not slowing down. The teams that figure out how to integrate them into a disciplined system will ship faster with less regression. The teams that don't will generate a lot of design debt.

The bottleneck isn't the AI. It's the layer between the AI and the system — the guardrails, the token contract, the handoff definition. That layer requires design judgment and engineering knowledge at the same time. Most teams don't have both in one person. I do.
